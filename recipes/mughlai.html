Mughlai Paratha

Hii Everyone!!

Mughlai Paratha is an Indian dish that was invented back in the medieval era by Mughals.

This dish is commonly eaten in Northern regions like Delhi, West UP, Haryana and West Bengal.

It tastes delighful! Filled with scrambled eggs and potato..people of all ages love it!!

This is how you make it:

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)

For the Filling:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup cooked minced chicken or mutton (optional)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough:

In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and oil.

Add water gradually and knead into a soft dough.

Cover and let it rest for 20â€“30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling:

In a bowl, beat the eggs.

Add chopped onions, green chilies, coriander, spices, and salt.

Mix in the cooked minced meat (if using).

3. Assemble the Paratha:

Divide the dough into medium-sized balls.

Roll out each ball into a thin square or round sheet.

Heat a flat pan or tawa on medium flame.

Place the rolled dough on the pan and pour the egg mixture into the center.

Fold the sides like an envelope to seal the filling inside.

Drizzle some oil around it and cook on medium heat until golden brown and crisp on both sides.

Serving Suggestion:

Serve hot with ketchup, chutney, or a side of salad and raita.
